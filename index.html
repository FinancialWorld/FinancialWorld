Definitions

The coupon rate, also know as the interest rate, is the nominal payment paid periodically by the issuer relative to the bond's nominal value.

Payments occur at predetermined time frequency. It can be annual, semiannual, quarterly, monthly, weekly depending on the bond features. The frequency is constant over the life of the security.

The market yield of a bond is the current yield that investors are willing to pay or sell to get or divest the security. The market yield of the bond (also said the Yield-to-Maturity) depends on the current market's conditions (interest rates, liquidity, ect) and the issuer's credit quality (knows also as credit spread).

The dirty price also named the full coupon price is the price that a buyer must pay to afford it. This price includes the accrued interests since the last coupon payment. These interests are due to the seller.

The clean price is the price without the accrued interests. This price make easier the comparison of bonds costliness
